
# Soundscape Atlas — Future Development Ideas

This document outlines potential features and design directions for the next stages of development.

These ideas can be implemented gradually as the platform evolves.

---

# Major Future Features

## 1. Compare Recordings

Allow users to compare recordings from the same site across time.

Example use case:

Imbota Creek

- Aug 2024 — 06:00
- Aug 2023 — 06:00
- Aug 2022 — 06:00

Users could switch between them instantly.

This would make seasonal and yearly comparisons extremely powerful.

---

## 2. Detection Timeline Expansion

The detection timeline could evolve into a full soundscape activity visualization.

Example display:

▒▒▒▒░░▒▒▒▒▒▒░░░▒▒

This would communicate activity density across the hour.

Users could also click detections to jump directly to that moment.

---

## 3. Species Explorer

Add a species exploration interface.

Example queries:

- find all recordings containing Superb Fairywren
- listen to magpie dawn chorus across locations
- map species detections geographically

This could transform the platform into a powerful ecological listening tool.

---

## 4. Seasonal Listening Mode

Allow users to explore recordings by ecological season.

Possible presets:

- Spring dawn chorus
- Summer insects
- Winter quiet forest
- Autumn migration

This would create curated listening experiences.

---

## 5. Acoustic Activity Map

Generate an acoustic richness score for each site based on:

- species detections
- detection density
- acoustic diversity metrics

Markers could visually represent:

- quiet environments
- highly active soundscapes

---

## 6. Listening Journeys

Create curated soundscape journeys.

Example:

“Morning in an Australian riparian forest”

Users move through recordings in sequence to experience changing soundscapes.

---

# Data Visualization Ideas

## Acoustic Calendar

Visualize activity across the year.

Example:

Month vs hour heatmap showing bird activity levels.

---

## Soundscape Fingerprints

Each recording could display a simple acoustic signature:

- spectral density
- call activity
- silence periods

---

# Community Features

These features can be introduced later.

## Listener Comments

Users could leave observations such as:

- species heard
- unusual events
- seasonal notes

---

## Support and Donations

A simple “support this project” option could help fund field recordings.

---

## Download Options

Offer:

- free compressed listening versions
- paid high‑resolution downloads

This can support long‑term sustainability.

---

# Immersive Interface Ideas

## Playback Halo on Map

When audio plays, the map marker could emit a subtle pulse.

This visually links sound with place.

---

## Time of Day Map Colours

Map markers could change colour based on recording time:

- blue — night
- orange — dawn
- yellow — day
- purple — dusk

---

# Long‑Term Vision

The goal of Soundscape Atlas is to become a **global listening archive of natural environments**.

Over time the platform could support:

- thousands of recordings
- hundreds of sites
- long‑term ecological monitoring
- public engagement with natural soundscapes

The design philosophy should always remain:

audio first, data second.
